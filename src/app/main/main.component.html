About

Welcome! I am a PhD student in the Department of Political Science at Simon Fraser University and a Joseph-Armand Bombardier Doctoral Fellow. 

I specialize in interest group lobbying, methodology, and Canadian politics. My dissertation employs data science techniques to generate new knowledge about how interest groups interact with and influence politicians. 

On this website you can find my CV and links to some of my current work. You can also contact me here.

Research

In my dissertation, I consider the relationship between information, interest groups, and institutions. I do so in the context of popular concerns over democratic responsiveness, government accountability, and political polarization. Specifically, I explore whether interest groups have a moderating effect on the link between voter attention and government attention.

I argue that certain groups – namely, public interest groups – can strengthen the link between voter attention and government attention. However, I also explore whether this comes at a cost in the legislature. Given disciplined parties, interest groups may weaken the legislative branch’s capacity to hold the executive accountable. Moreover, strong ties between interest groups and political parties may result in polarized, asymmetric access to information in Parliament.
